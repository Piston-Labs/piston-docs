Key People - Deep Dives

Laura Good - The Hub

"I'd rather see fifty $20 million dollar companies grow here. I think it would be better overall for our economy."

  • Role: CEO & Co-founder, StartupSac
  • Background: Worked at tech startup from early stage to exit. In Sacramento ecosystem since 2008.
  • Also: Consulting for Sacramento Angels, Marketing at CleanStart, Startup Weekend organizer, Startup Digest curator
  • Education: Santa Clara University
  • Philosophy: Grassroots community building over unicorn hunting. Values founders who create local jobs.
  • Approach: She knows everyone. Ask for specific introductions after establishing rapport.

Mark Haney - The Media Mogul

"HaneyBiz is designed to help entrepreneurs be entrepreneurs by providing one-stop access to capital, expertise, and a strong community."

  • Role: CEO, HaneyBiz; Founding Partner, Growth Factory
  • Background: Video rental (12 locations) → Surveillance tech ($400M revenue, 300 employees) → Led merger of two largest US security distributors
  • Portfolio: 50+ Sacramento companies across media, tech, consumer, real estate, construction, transportation
  • Media: The Mark Haney Podcast - attracts NorCal millionaires, billionaires, founders
  • Gives Back: Past President of EO Sacramento, Allegiant Giving (military veterans)
  • Approach: Lead with your founder story. He's drawn to scrappy entrepreneurs who've built real things.

Lokesh Sikaria - The VC

"From his humble beginnings in Assam, India to his current mission of transforming Sacramento's funding landscape."

  • Role: Founder & Managing Partner, Moneta Ventures
  • Background: UC Berkeley EECS. Started career at PwC San Francisco.
  • Exits: Founded Sparta Consulting ($100M+ in 5 years, acquired by KPIT Cummins). COO at Rapidigm ($250M revenue, acquired by Fujitsu 2006).
  • Fund: Started with $25M in 2014, now $250M+ across 45+ companies
  • Focus: B2B software, enterprise customers - reflects his operational background
  • Approach: He's an operator turned investor. Emphasize your unit economics and enterprise sales potential.

Rick Spencer - The Pre-Seed Guy

  • Role: Managing Partner, Growth Factory Ventures
  • Background: Startup founder/exec with exit (Briotix - sold to public company)
  • Mission: Help 5,000 founders launch companies by 2026
  • Fund I: 70+ startups in Big Data, Cloud, B2B SaaS, AI
  • Fund II: Launching Q4 2025, includes venture studio
  • Approach: Perfect fit for your stage. Be direct about what you need and your traction.

Cameron Law - The Academic Hub

  • Role: Executive Director, Carlsen Center for Innovation & Entrepreneurship (Sac State)
  • Background: Former Executive Director of Social Venture Partners Sacramento. Hired at 27.
  • Runs: 1 Million Cups Sacramento, Global Entrepreneurship Week regional hub
